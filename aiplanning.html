About This Resource

This guide was created for busy teachers and tutors in the UK working with adult ESL learners. The focus is on creating practical, real-world materials that align with the requirements of qualifications like **Pearson Functional Skills English at Entry Levels 1 and 2**.

The aim is not to replace your professional skills and experience, but to provide you with a tool to handle the heavy lifting of resource creation. Think of it as a creative partner that can help you brainstorm ideas, draft worksheets, and design activities, allowing you to focus more on what you do best: teaching and supporting your learners.

Resource Example 1: Entry 1 Reading Activity

The Goal:

To give an Entry 1 learner practice in reading a simple, authentic text and finding specific information.

The Prompt:

"I am an ESL teacher for an **Entry 1 adult learner.** Please create a simple **worksheet.** The **task** is to read a very simple text message reminder for a dental appointment. Include the place, date, and time. Then, create 3 simple questions below the text message asking 'Where?', 'When?', and 'What time?'."

  3. What time is the appointment? ____________________

Resource Example 2: Entry 2 Speaking & Listening Role-Play

The Goal:

To give Entry 2 learners practice in a transactional conversation that requires asking for something and understanding a simple response.

The Prompt:

"I am a **Functional Skills English tutor.** I need an activity for my **Entry 2 learners.** Please create **two role-play cards** for a scenario at a local chemist. **The task** is for the customer to ask for something for a headache. The chemist doesn't have the usual brand but offers an alternative. The customer should then agree."

The AI-Generated Resource:

Role-Play: At the Chemist
Card A: Customer
  • You have a bad headache.
  • Go to the chemist.
  • Ask the assistant for a box of 'HeadAid' tablets.
  • Listen to the assistant. They don't have 'HeadAid'.
  • Agree to take the alternative they offer.
Card B: Chemist's Assistant
  • You are working at the chemist.
  • A customer will ask you for 'HeadAid' tablets.
  • Tell them: "Sorry, we don't have any 'HeadAid' at the moment."
  • Offer them an alternative: "But we have 'ParaCure'. It's very similar. Would you like to try that?"
