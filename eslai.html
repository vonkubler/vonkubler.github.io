Empowering Adult ESL Learners with Google AI

This interactive guide provides a practical framework for integrating free Google AI tools into your ESL teaching. Explore tools and activities designed to support learners from Pre-Entry to Level 1, with a focus on pedagogy, differentiation, and saving you valuable time. Click the bubbles on the chart or use the navigation to explore tools by skill.

Speaking & Listening

This section focuses on tools that provide learners with safe, non-judgmental environments for oral practice. The activities are designed to build confidence, improve pronunciation, and develop conversational fluency through repeated, low-stakes interaction with AI. This supports the Communicative Language Teaching (CLT) approach by making authentic speaking and listening practice more accessible.

Google Assistant

+

Key Activity: Conversational Practice

Students engage in spoken dialogues with Google Assistant on their phones. This provides authentic, low-stakes practice, reducing anxiety and encouraging active language production.

  • Pedagogy: Fosters a psychologically safe environment for experimentation, crucial for adult learners who fear making mistakes. Directly supports CLT by providing on-demand conversational practice.
  • Scaffolding: Start with simple commands ("What's the weather?") and formulaic questions. Provide learners with a list of potential questions to ask.
  • Differentiation: Pre-Entry learners practice single questions. E3/Level 1 learners use "Continued Conversation" mode for multi-turn dialogues on topics like making reservations or asking for directions.
  • Stretch Target: Challenge Level 1 learners to maintain a conversation for several turns, ask follow-up questions, and use more complex sentence structures.

Google Docs Voice Typing

+

Key Activity: Dictation & Pronunciation Practice

Students speak into Google Docs, which transcribes their speech. They can then see any discrepancies between what they said and what the AI understood, providing immediate feedback on pronunciation and clarity.

  • Pedagogy: Bridges the gap between productive skills (speaking and writing). The immediate visual feedback enhances metacognitive awareness and facilitates autonomous self-correction.
  • Scaffolding: Provide simple, repetitive sentences for Pre-Entry learners to dictate. Model the process clearly.
  • Differentiation: Pre-Entry learners focus on single words/phrases. E3/Level 1 learners dictate full paragraphs and check for errors in sentence stress and intonation.
  • Stretch Target: Introduce voice commands for editing and formatting text (e.g., "new paragraph," "delete last word") for Level 1 learners.

Speechify (Chrome Extension)

+

Key Activity: Listening Comprehension with Control

Turns any digital text into audio. Students can listen to articles, stories, or their own writing read aloud, controlling the speed and voice to match their comprehension level.

  • Pedagogy: Supports Universal Design for Learning by providing multiple means of representation. Turns reading material into dynamic listening practice, ideal for blended learning and auditory learners.
  • Scaffolding: Start with simple texts at a slow speed. Pre-teach key vocabulary before listening.
  • Differentiation: Pre-Entry learners use slower speeds. E3/Level 1 learners can listen to more complex texts at faster speeds and with different accents.
  • Stretch Target: Challenge Level 1 learners to summarize a text they've only listened to, without reading the written version, to build auditory processing skills.

Reading

This section introduces AI tools that make authentic texts more accessible and help build crucial reading sub-skills. The focus is on reducing cognitive load, enabling learners to engage with meaningful content, and fostering independent reading strategies. These tools are essential for differentiation in mixed-ability classrooms.

Google Translate

+

Key Activity: Instant Vocabulary Support & Gist Reading

Use the Chrome extension or mobile camera (Lens) to get instant translations of unknown words, or translate entire webpages to understand the main ideas before tackling the English version.

  • Pedagogy: Acts as a comprehension scaffold, not a crutch. It reduces frustration, builds background knowledge (schema), and allows learners to stay engaged with challenging texts.
  • Scaffolding: Teach students to use it for key words, not every word. Use the full-page translate to get the gist, then switch back to English to read for detail.
  • Differentiation: Pre-Entry/E3 learners may rely on it more heavily. Level 1 learners should be encouraged to infer meaning from context first, using translate only for confirmation.
  • Stretch Target: Have students save new words to the Phrasebook, export to Google Sheets, and create digital flashcards on Quizlet for active recall practice.

Brisk Teaching (Chrome Extension)

+

Key Activity: Differentiated Reading Passages

Instantly change the reading level of any online text. This allows every student in a mixed-ability class to access the same core content at a level appropriate for them.

  • Pedagogy: A "game changer" for differentiation. Saves immense teacher preparation time and ensures all learners can engage with meaningful content at their "just right" level, fostering confidence.
  • Scaffolding: Provide a leveled-down version first for all students to ensure baseline comprehension.
  • Differentiation: Automatically creates different versions of a text for Pre-Entry, E3, and Level 1 learners from a single source article.
  • Stretch Target: Ask Level 1 learners to compare a leveled-down version with the original text and identify the specific changes in vocabulary and sentence structure.

Google Search

+

Key Activity: Building Background Knowledge & Vocabulary

Students use Google Search to research topics, find definitions, see example sentences, and use image search to understand abstract concepts before or during reading.

  • Pedagogy: Promotes learner autonomy and develops critical digital literacy skills. Building background knowledge (schema) is essential for deep reading comprehension.
  • Scaffolding: Provide specific search prompts (e.g., "definition of democracy," "what does a barrister do?"). Model how to use image search for vocabulary.
  • Differentiation: Pre-Entry learners can search in their native language first or focus on image searches. Level 1 learners can research topics in English.
  • Stretch Target: Teach Level 1 students to evaluate search results for credibility and bias, synthesizing information from multiple sources.

Writing

This section highlights tools that support the writing process, from generating ideas to drafting and revision. The focus is on providing immediate, non-judgmental feedback to encourage practice and on tools that help teachers provide more targeted, efficient feedback. This fosters an iterative approach to writing, where mistakes are seen as learning opportunities.

Lingually AI (Chrome Extension)

+

Key Activity: Real-time Grammar Correction

Provides instant grammar corrections and suggestions as students type in their browser (e.g., emails, social media). This is ideal for low-stakes, informal writing practice.

  • Pedagogy: Creates a safe space for writing practice by providing immediate, private feedback. This reduces the fear of making mistakes and encourages learners to write more frequently.
  • Scaffolding: Encourage use for short, informal tasks first, like writing a comment or a short email.
  • Differentiation: Pre-Entry learners focus on sentence-level accuracy. E3/Level 1 learners can use the feedback to work on more complex grammar and sentence structure.
  • Stretch Target: Ask Level 1 learners to analyze *why* a correction was suggested, promoting deeper grammatical understanding rather than blind acceptance.

Brisk Teaching (for Teachers)

+

Key Activity: Personalized Feedback & Process Inspection

Teachers use Brisk to generate high-quality, personalized feedback ("Glow & Grow," "Next Steps") directly in Google Docs. The "Inspect Writing" feature shows a playback of the student's writing process.

  • Pedagogy: Saves teachers significant time while improving the quality and specificity of feedback. "Inspect Writing" provides a unique window into the learning process, allowing for highly targeted interventions and supporting academic integrity.
  • Scaffolding: Use the "Create Content" feature to generate writing prompts or sentence starters for learners.
  • Differentiation: The feedback generated can be tailored to different proficiency levels and learning goals.
  • Stretch Target: Use the "Inspect Writing" feature with Level 1 students to facilitate a metacognitive discussion about their own writing and revision strategies.

Google Docs

+

Key Activity: Collaborative Writing & Guided Templates

Students work together in real-time to co-author documents, providing peer feedback. Teachers can create templates with sentence starters or outlines to guide the writing process.

  • Pedagogy: Promotes writing as a social, communicative, and iterative process. Peer feedback and negotiation of meaning are powerful learning tools.
  • Scaffolding: Provide clear templates with structured outlines, word banks, and sentence starters for Pre-Entry/E3 learners.
  • Differentiation: Group students of mixed abilities for peer support. Assign different roles within the collaborative writing task.
  • Stretch Target: Challenge Level 1 students to provide detailed, constructive feedback to their peers using the comment feature, referencing a shared rubric.

Assessment & Feedback

This final section covers how AI tools can make assessment more efficient and feedback more effective. The focus is on using technology to automate formative checks, provide immediate remediation, and gain insights into student progress, allowing teachers to focus on responsive teaching and targeted support.

Google Forms

+

Key Activity: Automated Quizzes

Create self-grading quizzes for grammar, vocabulary, and reading/listening comprehension. This provides immediate feedback to students and valuable data for the teacher.

  • Pedagogy: Supports formative assessment by providing instant feedback, which is a powerful learning aid. The automatic grading frees up teacher time to analyze results and plan interventions.
  • Scaffolding: Use images and embed audio/video clips to make questions more accessible for lower-level learners.
  • Differentiation: Create quizzes with varying levels of difficulty. Pre-Entry quizzes can be picture-based, while Level 1 quizzes can assess inference and nuance.
  • Stretch Target: In the quiz feedback settings, add links to explanatory videos or articles for incorrect answers, providing immediate, personalized remediation for Level 1 students.

Google Classroom Originality Reports

+

Key Activity: Plagiarism Detection & Citation Practice

Enable originality reports on writing assignments to compare student work against web pages and books, helping to teach academic integrity and proper citation.

  • Pedagogy: Supports the development of crucial academic skills. Use reports not as a punitive tool, but as a teaching moment to discuss paraphrasing, summarizing, and citing sources.
  • Scaffolding: Model how to paraphrase and cite a source correctly before the assignment. Review an example originality report with the class.
  • Differentiation: Focus on basic "copy/paste" issues with E3 learners. Discuss more nuanced source integration with Level 1 learners.
  • Stretch Target: Have Level 1 students analyze their own originality report and write a short reflection on how they could improve their source integration in the future.
